Prevention

  • Healthy Lifestyle: Maintain a balanced diet low in saturated fats, sugars, and salt while high in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Regular exercise, at least 150 minutes per week, helps manage weight and promotes heart health.

  • Avoid Smoking and Excessive Alcohol: Smoking damages blood vessels and increases the risk of heart disease. Excessive alcohol consumption can elevate blood pressure and contribute to heart problems.

  • Regular Health Check-ups: Monitor blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and blood sugar regularly. Managing these within healthy ranges reduces the risk of heart disease.

  • Medications and Treatments: Depending on individual health factors, doctors may prescribe medications to control blood pressure, cholesterol, and other heart disease risk factors. In more severe cases, procedures like angioplasty, stent placement, or bypass surgery might be necessary.

  • Manage Stress: Stress can contribute to heart disease. Techniques such as meditation, yoga, or counseling can help manage stress levels.

  • Awareness and Education: Educating oneself about heart disease risk factors, symptoms, and healthy habits can significantly reduce the chances of developing heart-related issues.

  • Emergency Response Training: Learning CPR and basic first aid can save lives during a cardiac emergency.
